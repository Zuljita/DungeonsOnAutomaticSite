The pipeline

Three tiers. One coherent dungeon.

DOA doesn't scatter rooms at random. It allocates the big picture, shapes it organically, then fills in the stonework — so the result reads like a place that was built, settled, and abandoned in that order.

TIER 01

Partition the biomes

Binary space partitioning carves the level into rectangular biome regions — caves, fortress, flooded ruin — with full coverage and no gaps.

BSP · macro allocation
TIER 02

Grow the zones

Organic growth reshapes each biome into natural, irregular zones with their own themes, tags and danger — and decides how they meet at the seams.

Organic · zone shaping
TIER 03

Collapse the rooms

Wave function collapse fills each zone with rooms, corridors and walls, then deterministic passes place encounters, traps, treasure and keys.

WFC · room detail
What you get

A whole GM prep packet — not just a map.

Feature parity with the dungeon you'd spend a Saturday drawing, minus the Saturday.

GM & player maps

Secret doors, traps and hidden features on the GM map; a clean, player-safe version with the secrets stripped out.

Source-book filtering

Toggle the books you own. The engine treats the filtered pool as the only truth — no surprise monsters from a supplement you don't have.

Danger by density

Tune Party CER and a challenge profile, from Cakewalk to Evil. Threat is a probability weave, not a budget — difficulty emerges.

Traps, treasure & keys

Every room rolls against its own peril for encounters, hazards, loot and quest keys — placed, tagged, and ready to read.

Lock & reroll

Keep the rooms that work, reroll the ones that don't. Deterministic sub-seeds mean a generator, not a slot machine.

Export everywhere

Foundry scenes, Universal VTT with consolidated walls, player-safe PNGs, and Markdown room keys for prep.
