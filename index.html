The pipeline

Three tiers. One coherent dungeon.

DOA does not scatter rooms at random. It allocates the big picture, shapes it organically, then fills in the stonework so the output reads like a place with history.

TIER 01

Partition the biomes

Binary space partitioning carves the level into complete biome regions: caves, fortress, flooded ruin, crypt, and whatever else the day has done to deserve this.

BSP · macro allocation
TIER 02

Grow the zones

Organic growth turns rigid regions into irregular zones with their own tags, danger pressure, and map logic.

Organic · zone shaping
TIER 03

Collapse the rooms

Wave function collapse places rooms, corridors, walls, and constraints, then deterministic passes dress the dungeon for play.

WFC · room detail

What you get

A whole GM prep packet.

A runnable dungeon should include more than rectangles. DOA produces maps, notes, encounter context, export files, and enough structure for you to improvise confidently.

MAPS

GM and player views

Secret doors, traps, hidden features, and notes stay on the GM side. The player map is clean enough to put on the table.

BOOKS

Source filtering

Tell the engine what books you own. It builds from that pool instead of asking you to improvise around missing material.

THREAT

Danger by density

Party CER and challenge profile shape the probability field. Difficulty emerges room by room.

KEYS

Room keys

Rooms get encounters, hazards, treasure hooks, and dressing in a format meant for actual prep, not decorative screenshots.

CONTROL

Profiles and rerolls

Export/import generation profiles, persist last settings locally, and keep deterministic seeds stable while you revise.

EXPORT

VTT-ready output

Foundry scene JSON and Universal VTT files include map imagery, walls, portals, lights, and GM/player modes. Importer QA is ongoing.

GEOMETRY

DFRPG scale

Maps use 1-foot cell geometry with a 1-yard DFRPG hex overlay, so room shapes can stay tactical without losing table scale.

INTENT

Dungeon Intent

Brief Markdown intent, global tag bias, region tag bias, and set-piece requests help steer the generator without hand-authoring every room.

POLISH

Offline or local AI

Use no-model sample polish for offline text cleanup, or optional local-only LLM polish through Ollama, LM Studio, or llama.cpp.

Why it exists

Built to break the rut.

An infinite-content engine for the table: point it at a seed and a set of constraints and it produces a complete, coherent dungeon — and it can do that forever, never the same way twice. The aim is range no hand-keyed campaign can keep up with.

SNOWFLAKE

Reproducible bones, one-off soul

Deterministic code lays the skeleton from seed, settings, and source books — the same bones every time. Then the local directors polish it into a one-off. Reroll and you get a different good dungeon, never that exact one again. Love what you got? Keep the output.

RANGE

Make character points matter

Varied light, sanctity, mana, terrain, and traversal mean the things your players paid for actually come up. Night Vision earns its cost, casters feel the field shift, and the table stops seeing the same dungeon in new paint.

ZERO PREP

Beer-and-pretzels, on demand

Sometimes the table just wants to show up and crawl. One click turns that into a complete, runnable dungeon in seconds — for the physical table or, through the Foundry and Universal VTT exports, the digital one.
